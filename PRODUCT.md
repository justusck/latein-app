# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single, motivated self-learner of Latin (the app's author and primary user),
studying on a phone — on the couch, on a train, in spare minutes — fully offline.
They want to build real reading ability in classical Latin, not collect points.
Sessions are short and frequent: review the words due today, drill a grammar
paradigm, ask the AI a question, read a passage. The job on any given screen is to
get into the actual study task with as little friction as possible and a clear
sense of what to do next.

## Product Purpose

Latīna is an offline-first Latin learning app built around evidence-based method:
FSRS spaced repetition for vocabulary and grammar, frequency-ordered vocabulary
portioning (DCC order), a grammar-first skill tree, and reading texts that unlock
once ~90% of their vocabulary is known. An on-device AI tutor (Claude, user's own
key) and a reading view are fed by a derived "knowledge" model of what the learner
has actually mastered. Success is sustained daily practice that measurably grows
the learner's known vocabulary and grammar coverage, and the ability to read real
Latin texts with less and less help.

## Brand Personality

Confident and classical. Roman-imperial gravitas: parchment and ink, terracotta,
imperial purple, and gold — a little ceremonial, never dusty. The voice is German,
direct, and quietly expert: it states what to do and what was learned, without
cheerleading or jargon. Gamification (XP, levels, streak, coins) is present but
earns its place as a moment of acknowledgment, not the personality of the whole
interface. The feeling to evoke: studying from a beautifully made classical
textbook that happens to know exactly what you need next.

## Anti-references

- **Cartoony Duolingo.** No mascots, bouncy blobs, confetti storms, or childish
  illustration. Gamification stays grown-up and restrained.
- **Generic SaaS dashboard.** No cold blue/gray, no hero-metric template, no
  Inter-everything corporate-tool look.
- **Dusty academic PDF.** Not dense, gray, joyless walls of unstyled text like an
  old grammar reference. Scholarship without the dust.
- **Flashy crypto/AI app.** No neon gradients, decorative glassmorphism, or
  dark-mode-hype aesthetic. The AI tutor is a calm helper, not a spectacle.

## Design Principles

- **Method made visible.** The science (frequency order, spaced-repetition due
  counts, knowledge-gated reading) should be legible in the UI, so the learner
  trusts that the next thing shown is the right next thing. Show the reasoning,
  don't just assert progress.
- **Into the task, fast.** Every screen leads to a single obvious action: start
  studying, drill this paradigm, ask, read. Minimize taps and chrome between
  intent and practice.
- **Classical, not costume.** The Roman palette and weighty type are the identity;
  use them with restraint so they read as a confident textbook, not a theme park.
- **Acknowledge, don't celebrate.** Progress and rewards are stated plainly and
  given a brief, dignified moment — never a constant stream of praise.
- **Earned familiarity.** Standard mobile affordances (tabs, lists, sheets, native
  controls) behave exactly as users expect; surprise is reserved for content and
  moments, never for how a button or form works.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal compliance target (personal single-user app), but keep it sensible:
maintain readable contrast against the parchment and night backgrounds, support
both light and dark themes, and keep tap targets comfortable. The existing classical
palette is the constraint to design within. Revisit if the app is ever distributed
more widely.
